Form Validator

In this exercise, you're going to do some simple form validation and, if everything checks out, display a confirmation message.

Part One: Form validation

The first part is pretty easy: create a sign-up form that looks roughly like this:

The form should accept a Name, Email address, and a Confirmation email address, and have a checkbox to Sign up for spam.

Part Two: Validation

When the user click Sign me up!, your code should validate the form. There are four rules:

  1. Name cannot be empty
  2. Email cannot be empty
  3. Confirmation email cannot be empty
  4. Confirmation email must match Email

Part Three: Confirmation

If the form passes validation, you should display a confirmation message. The first line should say:

Congratulations, ${Name}!

Our incredibly valuable content is on its way to your inbox as we speak!

Below that, show a custom message, based on whether or not they signed up for Spam. If they did, display:

And so is a ton of junk from our cohorts valued partners!

But if they don't sign up for Spam, give them a moment to regret it:

But are you sure you don't want those special offers?
